Subject: Request for meeting in regard to sale of household insectcide. 
Hon’ble Joint Secretary, 
Greetings from Retailers Association of India (RAI), 
Retailers Association of India (RAI), is the premier-most body representing modern 
retailers across the country. Our membership base includes over 900 retail organization 
which comprise of over 298000 store outlets. RAI has constantly strived for conducive 
regulation and legislation that promotes the interests of consumers by allowing modern 
retail to grow and flourish. It is in this interest that we write to you today to express some 
of the issues faced by our members engaged in the sale of household insecticides. 
As you would be aware, the subject of import, manufacture, sale, transport, distribution 
and use of insecticides is regulated by the Insecticides Act, 1968. The noble aim of the act 
is to ensure that risk arising from misuse of insecticides towards human and animal life 
is mitigated by regulation. We agree that considering the harmful or potentially harmful 
effects of insecticides it is important that some of them be tightly regulated to prevent 
any harm.  
However, we would like to submit to you that considering the overbearing nature of this 
regulation, it poses certain impediments to the retail industry which we believe can be 
addressed without increasing the risk exposure. Most retail outlets are engaged in the 
sale of “household insecticides” which pose considerably less risk to life as compared to 
other industrial or commercial insecticides which indeed need to be regulated more 
strictly. We recommend you to reconsider rule 9 (4B) of new amendment in insecticide 
rules 1971.  
Rule 9 (4B) of new amendment in insecticide rules 1971 
A person who applies for the grant of license to sell, stock or exhibit for sale or distribute 
insecticides shall possess or shall employ a person possessing a graduate degree in 
Agricultural Sciences or in Science with Chemistry or Botany or Zoology as the minimum 
qualification: Provided that all retailers or dealers possessing a valid license as on the date 
of notification of this sub-rule shall be allowed a period of two years to comply with the 
educational qualifications. 
RAI views and recommendation 
1. Household insecticides are great means to fight disease, and this clause will 
prohibit their free sale and use. 
 
2. The new compliance requirement of a mandatory science degree qualification 
will adversely impact the business of retailers and pose a threat to

In summary, we submit that there needs to be a classification of insecticides on the basis 
of risk posed and selling of household insecticide should not be subject to rule 9 (4 B). 
The government needs to consider relooking if this requirement is necessary. This will 
help retailers to satisfy consumer needs and sell ‘safe for use house use insecticides’ such 
as mosquito coils, insect killers etc. The removal of this requirement will also allow the 
authorities to focus on more harmful insecticides that pose a much greater risk.
