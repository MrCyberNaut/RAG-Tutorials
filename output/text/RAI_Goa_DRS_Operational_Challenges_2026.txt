Greetings from Retailers Association of India (RAI)! 
 
It is my pleasure to write to you on behalf of RAI.   
  
RAI is the unified voice of Indian retailers working with all the stakeholders for creating the right 
environment for the growth of the modern retail industry in India. It encourages, develops, 
facilitates and supports retailers to modernise and adopt best practices that will delight 
customers. RAI is the premier body of Retailers in India, having around 14000-member 
establishments, including large and small retailers in the country, representing approximately 
700,000 retail stores. 
 
RAI , representing organised and Modern retail businesses across the State of Goa, would like to 
place on record its views and concerns regarding the proposed implementation of the Deposit 
Refund System (DRS) with effect from 2nd April 2026.  
 
RAI fully supports the Government’s objective of reducing plastic pollution and promoting 
environmentally responsible consumption. However, based on feedback received from retailers 
across formats—kirana stores, supermarkets, beverage outlets, and modern trade— we foresee 
significant operational, financial, and compliance challenges arising from the current 
implementation framework and timeline. 
 
 
Key Challenges Faced by Retailers  
 
1. State-Specific Implementation  
Since DRS is being implemented only in Goa, many manufacturers are hesitant to introduce 
Goa-specific packaging. This may result in supply shortages, restricted product availability, 
and loss of consumer choice at retail outlets. 
 
2. Inflow of Non-DRS Stock  
Retailers located near state borders are particularly vulnerable to inadvertent inflow of non-
QR-coded stock from neighbouring states, exposing them to penalties despite lack of intent 
or control.

3. Working Capital Impact  
The additional ₹5 deposit per unit significantly increases inventory value at retail level, 
especially for high-turnover beverage categories, putting pressure on cash flows and margins.  
 
4. Stock Transition Issues  
The requirement to withdraw existing stock on 31st March, re-sticker it, and reintroduce it 
on 1st April is practically unviable for retailers, especially small and medium stores with 
limited space and manpower. 
 
5. QR Code & Sticker Reliability  
Retailers will face customer dissatisfaction due to:  
 
• QR codes not scanning  
• Stickers getting damaged or falling off  
• Disputes at billing and refund points  
 
Retailers have no control over sticker quality, yet may be held accountable. 
 
6. Risk of Penal Action  
The proposed penalty of ₹25,000 per incident for any non-compliant item is extremely harsh 
for retailers and may lead to fear-driven business disruptions, especially during the initial 
phase.  
 
7. System & Billing Readiness  
Retailers’ POS and accounting systems require substantial modification to accommodate DRS 
deposits. Many small retailers depend on third-party vendors who may not be ready by the 
cut-off date, risking billing paralysis.  
 
8. Consumer Awareness Gap  
In the absence of widespread public education, retailers will bear the brunt of consumer 
confusion, disputes, and dissatisfaction, affecting store operations and customer trust. 
 
 
Our Humble Requests 
 
In light of the above, RAI respectfully requests the Government to consider the following: 
 
• Phased or pilot implementation of DRS before full-scale rollout 
• Extended transition period with a grace window for compliance 
• Temporary relaxation of penalties during the initial implementation phase 
• Clear liability demarcation between manufacturers, distributors, and retailers 
• State-led consumer awareness campaigns prior to enforcement 
• Allowing sell-through of pre-DRS stock without penal consequences

Conclusion 
 
Retailers are committed partners in achieving Goa’s environmental goals. However, a 
collaborative, practical, and phased approach to DRS implementation will ensure 
environmental success without disrupting retail operations, livelihoods, and consumer 
access. 
 
In view of the significant operational, technological, and supply-chain challenges outlined 
above, the Retailers Association of India (RAI) respectfully urges the Government of Goa to 
defer the implementation of the proposed Deposit Refund System (DRS) until after December 
2026. 
 
We request an opportunity to engage in a stakeholder consultation to discuss these issues in 
detail and work towards a mutually viable solution. 
 
We thank you for your time and consideration.
