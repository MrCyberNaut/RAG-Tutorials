Shri Santosh Lad, 
Hon’ble Minister of Labour, 
Government of Karnataka. 
 
Subject: Impact of Karnataka Minimum Wages Notification dated 30th December 
2017. 
Hon’ble Minister, 
Greetings from Retailers Association of India (RAI)! 
Retailers Association of India (RAI) is the unified voice of Indian retailers working with 
all the stakeholders for creating the right environment for the growth of the modern 
retail industry in India. RAI is the body that encourages, develops, facilitates and 
supports retailers to become modern and adopt best practices that will delight 
customers. RAI has a three charter aim of Retail Development, Facilitation and 
Propagation. RAI is the premier body of Retailers in India having more than 1000 
member establishments including large and small retailers in the country having 
approximately 500,000 stores. 
We are writing this letter to express our concerns on Karnataka Minimum Wages 
Notification dated 30th December 2017. Please find the notification attached 
herewith. The notification accounts for an increase in minimum wages by 53% 
percent. By doing so, following issues in the market will be created as far as the Retail 
Industry is concerned: 
1. Retail Industry is manpower intensive, uses moderately educated youth and 
provides employment to a large number of manpower who are often 
beginning of their careers. 
 
2. The substantial hike in minimum wages across the state will eventually 
render labour-intensive business like retail economically unviable. In a high 
labour-intensive retail industry, this will inflict high costs without any returns 
thereof, and will only cause an increase in the cost of goods and services, 
which eventually will have to be passed to the public at large. 
 
3. The Retail sector is the highest employment generating sector in the State. 
Since the sector accounts for creating maximum employment and operates 
on marginal viability, a substantial increase in wages is not sustainable and 
may see the closure of many small and tiny retail enterprises and loss of 
employment will be the direct outcome. 
 
4. The move may force many retailers to shift out their business from 
Karnataka to neighbouring states.
