RAI Members Advisory on GST 2.0 Rate Rationalisation 
 
Background 
The Government of India has introduced GST 2.0 rate rationalisation, effective 22nd 
September 2025, under which goods and services are broadly categorized into two slabs 
– 5% and 18%. 
In line with the principles of anti-profiteering under GST law, businesses are obligated to 
pass on any reduction in tax incidence to consumers. Conversely, where GST rates have 
increased, appropriate revisions to Maximum Retail Price (MRP) can be undertaken 
transparently. 
 
RAI advises its members to adopt standardised communication and compliance practices 
in both scenarios to maintain consumer trust, regulatory compliance, and smooth store 
operations. 
 
At the heart of GST 2.0 implementation lies the principle of customer centricity. Retailers 
must ensure that consumers are clearly and transparently informed about any change 
in prices arising from GST rate rationalisation. Transparent communication not only 
ensures compliance but also strengthens consumer trust and confidence in modern 
retail. By proactively informing customers—whether through in-store displays, digital 
platforms, or billing disclosures—members can demonstrate their commitment to 
fairness, accountability, and customer-first values. 
 
Scenario 1: Decrease in GST Rate (e.g., 12% or 18% → 5%) 
Where GST rates have been reduced or input tax credit benefits become available,  
the benefit must be passed on to customers through a commensurate reduction in prices. 
 
 
 

 
 
Suggested Methods of Communication: 
1. In-store Communication 
• Posters and signage at entrances, shelves, and billing counters. 
• Highlight percentage discount due to GST reduction (e.g., “6.25% GST Savings on 
Garments under ₹2500”). 
• Showcase Before vs. After Prices on shelf labels. 
• Place comparative price charts (category/product-wise). 
Example Store Message: 
• Good News for Our Customers!   
• With the revised GST rates effective 22nd September 2025, we have reduced 
prices across categories. 
•  Your savings from GST reduction are being passed on directly to you. 
• Enjoy lower prices, same great quality! 
• Prices after GST revision are displayed on shelves and bills. 
• Look out for ‘Before & After GST Price’ tags for easy comparison.” 
2. Category / Shelf Signage 
• “Special GST Price Benefit! 
• Due to GST rate reduction from 12% → 5%, you now save 6.25% or more on 
garments under ₹2500. 
• Comparative price chart available for easy reference. 
     Old Price: ₹____ 
     New Price: ₹____ 
•  We pass on 100% of GST benefit to you.” 
 
 

 
 
3. Billing Counter / Checkout Message 
• “Prices reduced as per new GST rates effective 22nd September 2025. 
• GST benefit has been passed on to all our customers. 
• Check your bill for details!” 
4. Thematic Campaigns: 
Run customer-centric initiatives such as “GST Bachaav Utsav” to create awareness. 
 
5. Invoice-level Disclosure:  
Where technologically feasible, invoices should transparently reflect the GST benefit 
passed on to consumers. 
• Line-item Disclosure: Show Old MRP and New MRP (post-GST benefit). 
• Invoice Footer Note: “Prices reflect commensurate reduction due to GST rate 
revision effective 22 September 2025. GST benefit has been fully passed on to 
the customer.” 
6. Digital Communication: 
Inform customers via websites, mobile apps, SMS, and emails regarding reduced GST 
impact. 
 
Scenario 2: Increase in GST Rate (e.g., 12% → 18%) 
For goods packaged and labelled with pre-22 September 2025 MRPs, retailers are 
permitted to revise MRPs upwards to reflect GST rate changes. 
Guidelines for MRP Revision: 
1. Both MRPs must be visible – the old MRP cannot be erased or overwritten. 
2. Revised MRP must be indicated through sticker/stamp/printing, clearly stating the 
change is due to GST rate revision. 
3. Mention: “Revised MRP effective 22 September 2025 (due to GST rate change)”. 

 
4. The declaration should be legible, durable, and in the same or larger font size as 
the original MRP. 
5. Prominent in-store notices should inform customers about price increases due to 
GST rate revision. 
Note: Price increases due to GST revision may only be executed on or after 22nd 
September 2025.  
Brands/retailers may update MRPs at any time thereafter once they are confident of 
executing the changes and ensuring the revised prices are correctly displayed on 
products. 
Key Action Points for Members 
• Ensure immediate system updates to reflect revised tax rates in billing and ERP 
• Train store staff to communicate changes confidently to customers. 
• Maintain documentation of rate changes, communication material, and revised 
MRPs for compliance and audit purposes. 
• Using stickers on individual product is optional, not mandatory. However, in the 
event of a price increase, it is advisable to place stickers for the sake of 
transparency and to maintain customer trust. 
• Avoid practices of concealing revised MRPs or erasing earlier declarations. 
RAI recommends members adopt the above practices for consistent communication and 
compliance across the retail sector.  
Transparent communication will help avoid customer dissatisfaction, ensure compliance 
with GST regulations, and safeguard the reputation of modern retail.
