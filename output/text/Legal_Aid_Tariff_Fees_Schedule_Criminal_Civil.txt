Criminal Certificates      
 
Preamble 
The Tariff for criminal certificates aims to facilitate simple billing through a Block 
Fee system, or reasonable fixed rate compensation, for substantive steps taken 
to bring a certificate to conclusion. 
 
It is expected that the Extra Hours Block will not be invoiced as a matter of 
routine, but only for those rare and exceptional certificates that are inadequately 
compensated by the block fees seen below.  Time sheets will be required for any 
certificate that applies extra hours at the time of invoicing.   
 
Levels of Charges 
There are four levels of charges that will affect billing, described as follows: 
 
• Administrative Charges 
Consists of allegations of breaches, administration of justice offences, and 
offences charged under a provincial statute. 
 
• Summary Charges 
Consists of allegations in which the prosecution has proceeded summarily, 
the proceeding is hybrid and no Crown election has been made, or where the 
Criminal Code codifies that there is no election, and the matter is summary. 
 
• Indictable Charges 
Consists of allegations in which the prosecution has proceeded by Indictment 
or where the Criminal Code codifies that there is no election, and the matter is 
Indictable. 
 
• Major Charges 
Consists of allegations of offences detailed under Section 469 of the Criminal 
Code and other charges as detailed in LAA Administrative Policies. 
 
Our commitment to the Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC) Calls 
to Action 
Sections of this Tariff relating to Indigenous Courts and Gladue Reports 
represent Legal Aid Alberta’s commitment to respond to the TRC’s Calls to 
Action #30 and #38. We are consistently working to ensure equitable access to 
justice for Alberta’s Indigenous populations through the understanding and use of 
Indigenous laws, and the acknowledgement of unique cultures of Aboriginal 
peoples, not just in Alberta, but throughout Canada.

o This tariff item is billable when counsel appears in court to assist a

• Peace Bond 
o This tariff item is billable when counsel appears in court to assist a 
client in entering into a peace bond in any form. 
• Stay of Proceedings 
o This tariff item is billable when a stay is entered on all charges on an 
Information, and at least one appearance has been made and/or 
communications have taken place between counsel and the 
prosecution, resulting in the stay. 
• Withdrawal of Charges 
o This tariff item is billable when the prosecution withdraws on all 
charges on an Information, and at least one appearance has been 
made and/or communications have taken place between counsel 
and the prosecution, resulting in the stay. 
• Change of Counsel/Fail to Appear 
o This tariff item is billable when the solicitor/client relationship 
concludes due to the client failing to appear or a successful change 
of counsel application.  This must occur within 60 days of the start 
date of a scheduled trial or preliminary hearing. 
• Withdrawal of a Guilty Plea 
o This tariff item is billable when counsel appears in court and makes 
submissions with regards to the withdrawal of a guilty plea.
