Hon'ble Minister,  
 
Greetings from Retailers Association of India (RAI)! 
 
Retailers Association of India (RAI) is the unified voice of Indian retailers, working 
with all the stakeholders for creating the right environment for the growth of the 
modern retail industry in India. RAI is the body that encourages, develops, facilitates 
and supports retailers to become modern and adopt best practices that will delight 
customers. RAI has a three charter aim of Retail Development, Facilitation and 
Propagation. 
  
This representation has reference to the recent announcement made by the State 
Government imposing a ban on numerous plastic products. 
  
We, at RAI, welcome this initiative and believe that this is good for the environment. 
  
We would like to assure the Government that our members are committed to making 
positive efforts to control the use of plastic in a phased and focused manner and will 
not give Plastic Carry Bags to any customer. 
  
We would like to submit the following: 
  
1.     Plastic bags or plastic used to form an integral part of packaging 
should be exempted 
  
i)      We would like to highlight that under Rule 3 (b) of the Plastic Waste 
(Management and Handling) Rules, 2011, exemption is provided for 
plastic bags that constitute or form an integral part of packaging in 
which goods are sealed prior to use. 
  
ii)    There are “Freshness Bags/packages” which are used for hygienic 
reasons and enhance the shelf life of product sold in loose form  like Staples 
(Cereals, Pulses, Sugar, and Other grains), Fruits and Vegetables (procured 
every day from Framer and Mandis), Bakery Items ( Breads buns, and the 
Like), Meat, Fish and Chicken, Eggs.. Such packaging should not be

iii)   Often, these products are hand-picked by the consumer while shopping.

2.     Use of plastic packaging  should be allowed during transportation and

We request you to allow the use of plastic packages in transportation and 
storage of products. No country in the world has imposed a ban on the use of 
plastic in storage, handling, and transportation.  Other alternatives are not 
workable and will result in damage to goods like garments and apparel during 
transportation from one place to another. Garments are packed and stocked in 
a protective plastic to help them remain dust free. Retailers have bulk quantities 
of loose items like wheat, rice and cereals and pulses in warehouses, which are 
not for sale to the consumer. There is no better hygienic way to pack bulk 
material except in plastic bags.
