Greeting from Retailers Association of India! 
It is my pleasure to write on behalf of RAI.  
Retailers Association of India (RAI) is the unified voice of Indian retailers, 
working with all the stakeholders for creating the right environment for the 
growth of the modern retail industry in India. RAI is the body that encourages, 
develops, facilitates and supports retailers to become modern and adopt best 
practices that will delight customers.  
RAI is the representative body of Retailers in India having over 900 member 
establishments including large and small retailers in the country and 
represents over 98000 stores in India. RAI has a three charter aim of Retail 
Development, Facilitation and Propagation. 
We have prepared this representation to throw light on the repercussions of 
using paper bags towards the environment and the threats it poses to the 
natural resources.  
 
RAI had ,earlier represented to Government of India  to adopt  Polluter Pays 
Principle (PPP) for Plastic carry bags  to encourage prudent use of plastic 
carry bags and save the environment. The Polluter Pays Principle (PPP) is a 
widely used principle and implies that the cost incurred from any form of 
pollution should be the responsibility of the polluter in order to ensure that 
the environment is protected for present and future generations. The PPP is 
widely acknowledged as a general principle of International Environmental 
Law and is frequently mentioned in Multilateral Environmental Agreements.

The Ministry of Environment and Forests, accordingly, has issued a 
notification dated 4th February 2011 called the Plastic Waste (Management 
and Handling) Rules 2011. This notification is aimed at controlling the 
indiscriminate use of plastic and  it was mandated that no carry bags shall be 
made available free of cost by retailers to consumers. 
Under this notification various state prescribed charges for plastic bags to 
ensure its prudent use. There is a complete ban on plastic in the Delhi. 
 With a similar concern, we, at RAI believe that Paper Bags also relay an 
immense impact on the environment in terms of global warming, energy and 
water consumption, and waste generation. These factors lead to paper bags 
impacting the environment in a similar manner as plastic bags and hence need 
to be treated similarly. There is strong need to prevent the massive 
deforestation for manufacturing of paper bags as the compound annual 
growth rate of the Indian paper industry is growing at 11.7% as of 2014. Also, 
a paper bag in a landfill takes hundreds of years to degrade, similar to plastic 
bags in this case.

compared to plastic bags, which shows a lot of waste generation when paper

Apart from the factors mentioned above, there are other environmental 
hazards present when paper bags are used. Which range from rise in energy 
consumption to increase in GHGs (Green House Gasses). Some of the 
environmental concerns associated with the usage of paper bags are listed as 
follows: 
 Paper bags are largely manufactured from newly cut trees which leads 
to massive deforestation as the recycling process in India still only 
contributes to 1/4th of the total share of paper bags production. 
 Making a paper bag consumes approximately four times more energy 
than other carry bag options, showing increased fuel consumption. 
 Paper production emits air pollution, around 70 percent more than 
plastic bag production and in terms of greenhouse gasses, paper bags 
are reported to be emitting 80 percent more GHGs than its plastic 
counterpart. 
 Life cycle assessments have shown that manufacture of paper bags has 
a heavier environmental impact than its alternatives. 
 
Therefore, it has been observed that the environmental repercussions of using 
paper bags are also high and thus, the mandate for retailers to charge for carry 
bags should also include paper bags. The charge levied on paper bags will also 
help in spreading awareness among consumers regarding the environmental 
threats posed by the usage of paper bags and will further promote the reuse 
of these bags. 
The state may by circular determine the minimum price for paper  bags 
depending upon their quality and size which covers their material and waste 
management cost in order to encourage their re-use so as to minimize its 
consumption. 
Customers will only be charged the material cost of bags. They will also be 
given an option of bringing their own carry bags or reuse the bags provided
