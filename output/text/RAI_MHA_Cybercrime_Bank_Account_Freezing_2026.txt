instructions received from National Cyber Crime Police authorities, often without due 
process or proportionality.  
 
Sir, the whole activity of marking lien/freezing/ debiting the lien amount from the Bank account

place on record the evidences to show that the whole transaction that occasioned at the store or 
otherwise was a genuine sale transaction. You would appreciate that marking of liens/freezing of 
accounts/ refund of amount hampers the operations and leads to losses. Kindly consider the fact, 
that under good faith noting the authentication PIN/Code entered by the customer confirming 
the payment, the purchased goods are handed over to the customer, without further demur. Thus 
the Retailers are put to a great loss as not only they lose the amount but also lose the products 
which were sold from the store 
 
RAI and its members fully support the Government of India’s efforts to combat cybercrime and 
financial fraud. However, we are constrained to highlight that the current manner in which lien 
is being marked, debit of lien amount and account freezing is being implemented is causing 
severe and unintended hardship to bona fide retail businesses, many of which are victims 
rather than perpetrators of cyber fraud.

Key Issues Being Faced by Retailers 
 
1. Blanket Freezing of Entire Accounts: In several instances, banks have frozen entire 
current or collection accounts of retailers—even where the alleged disputed amount is 
minimal—thereby completely paralysing day-to-day business operations. 
2. Lack of Prior Notice or Opportunity to Respond: Retailers often receive no prior 
intimation, explanation, or opportunity to clarify facts before a lien is being marked on 
amount, debit of lien amount and their accounts are frozen. 
3. Disproportionate Impact on Operations: Frozen accounts prevent payment of salaries, 
statutory dues (GST, PF, ESI), vendor payments, rentals, and customer refunds, leading to 
reputational damage and operational disruption. 
4. Retailers as Collection Intermediaries: Most large retailers operate high-volume 
collection accounts where customer payments are routed through payment gateways. A 
single disputed transaction—often arising from fraud at the customer end—results in 
marking of lien or prior to release of the amount to the complainant’s account, freezing of 
the entire account balance, despite no wrongdoing by the retailer. 
5. Inconsistent Practices Across Banks: Banks appear to be acting mechanically on 
cybercrime instructions without applying independent judgment, risk assessment, or 
proportional safeguards. 
6. Denial of Natural Justice: The Retailers are not been provided an opportunity of 
representing to clarify facts before any lien is marked, release of lien amount into the 
complainant’s account and freezing of their accounts which is denial of principle of equity 
and natural justice. 
 
Judicial Guidance 
 
We wish to draw attention to the recent judgment of the Madras High Court in the case of V-
Mart Retail Limited, wherein the Hon’ble Court observed that: 
 
➢ Blanket freezing of bank accounts without quantifying the amount linked to alleged fraud 
is disproportionate 
➢ Bank accounts constitute property and cannot be restricted without adherence to due 
process and proportionality 
➢ Only the disputed amount, if any, may be lien-marked, and complete account freezing is 
not justified 
 
This judgment provides important guidance on balancing investigative needs with fundamental 
business rights.

Our Humble Requests 
 
In light of the above, RAI respectfully requests the Ministry of Home Affairs to consider issuing 
appropriate guidelines / standard operating procedures to cybercrime/Police authorities 
/banks/ Courts, including: 
 
1.  To give fair hearing to the account holder, before marking lien/ freezing of account / 
debiting Bank Account of the Retailers, in the interest of natural justice. 
2. Mandating quantification of the disputed amount before issuing account-freeze 
instructions 
3. Restricting freezes to the specific disputed amount, instead of the entire account balance 
4. Ensuring prior intimation and post-freeze communication to affected account holders 
5. Providing a time-bound review and grievance redressal mechanism 
6. Issuing clear guidance to banks to avoid mechanical, blanket freezes that disrupt 
legitimate businesses 
 
Such measures would significantly reduce collateral damage to compliant businesses while 
continuing to support effective cybercrime investigations. 
 
RAI and its members would be grateful for an opportunity to engage with the Ministry and 
relevant stakeholders to share industry perspectives and assist in developing a balanced and 
pragmatic framework. 
 
We thank you for your time and consideration.
