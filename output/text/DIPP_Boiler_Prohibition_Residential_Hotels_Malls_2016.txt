Dear Sir,
As you may be aware that the Central Boilers Board, constituted under the Boilers
Act, 1923 comprises of the representatives
of the Central and State Governments,
Bureau
of Indian Standards,
Boiler
and boiler component Manufacturers, National Laboratories,
Engineering Consultancy agencies, users of Boilers and other interests connected with the
Boiler Industry.
Secretary,
Department
of Industrial
Policy and Promotion
is the ex-
officio Chairman and Technical Adviser (Boilers) is the ex-officio Member-Secretary
of
the Board.
2. Board deals with the problems of both the users and manufacturers
and takes
policy decisions for proper growth of the boiler manufacturing
industry in the country.
Board
also
formulates
the
Indian
Boiler
Regulations
incorporating
the
latest
developments taking place in the Boiler Industry all over the world.
3. For the safety of life & property
from the danger of explosion of boilers, it is
proposed that boilers should be installed in a separate building and
these should not be
allowed to be installed
in buildings
where large number of persons
are present
like
residential buildings,
hotels, restaurants, hospitals, malls etc. However, boilers may be
allowed on roof top of buildings provided structure of building is fit for such installation.
This is proposed to
be made applicable to existing boiler installations
also for which
owners may be given two years' time for relocation of boilers.
4. Before
making
rules/regulations
for
implementing
the
proposal,
the
stakeholders
like
CBB
members/CIIIFICCIIASSOCHEM/FIEO/
Associations
for
Hotels/Malls/
Hospitals
etc. are requested to give their
comments/suggestions
on the

proposal
as
per
para
3
above.
The
stakeholders
are
requested
to
offer
their
comments/suggestions
within
a period of two months
from the date of issue of this
letter.
