Subject: Seeking reduction in GST levy on Job work in Textile sector 
 
Honorable Chairperson, 
Greetings from Retailers Association of India! 
It is my pleasure to write on behalf of Retailer’s Association of India.  
Retailers Association of India (RAI) is the unified voice of Indian retailers working with 
all the stakeholders for creating the right environment for the growth of the modern 
retail industry in India. RAI is the body that encourages, develops, facilitates and 
supports retailers to become modern and adopt best practices that will delight 
customers. RAI has a three charter aim of Retail Development, Facilitation and 
Propagation. RAI is the premier body of Retailers in India having around 1000 
member establishments including large and small retailers in the country having 
approximately 5,00,000 stores.  
 
We are writing to you in regard to the high GST levy on on stitching cloth/ Tailoring/ 
Job work of garments.  
 
While we welcome the governments decision regarding levy of GST on job work on 
textile yarns (other than man-made and filament) and textile fabrics to be brought 
down to 5 percent from 18 percent, but processes such as stitching, printing, 
embroidery are still subject to 18 percent, and not 5 percent, since the same are 
considered as ‘Service’. 
Our Submission:  
1. GST on readymade garments is between 5- 12%. Imposing a 18% GST rate on 
tailoring/ job work is very high. It is very clear that stitching cloth/tailoring is a 
‘subsequent process’ in the manufacture of textile products and not a service. 
We recommend that GST rate on stitching cloth/tailoring/Job work of garments 
should be brought down to 5 percentage from 18 percentage, similar to textile 
yarns (other than man-made and filament) and textile fabrics.

2. We would also like to draw your attention to the fact that 5 percentage GST levy 
is applicable on Jewellery & diamond processing & making charges of ornaments, 
inspite of the fact that Jewellery are luxury goods, whereas garments are basic 
necessities and essential commodities. If GST on labour is slabbed at 5% on labour 
charges on jewellery, which seems fair, the slab of 18% on tailoring is definitely an 
anomaly.  
 
3. Tailoring is definitely a necessity since people can survive without jewellery but 
not without clothing. Hence, it GST on about for tailoring should at best be equal 
to that in the jewellery industry if not lower. 
 
Tailoring has a rich heritage in India and imposing such a high GST rate on it would 
be a serious impediment to the "Make in India" mission of our Prime Minister. 
Furthermore, Tailoring is a labour intensive industry and due to high taxes, Karigar’s 
could loose out to readymade business therefore risking the livelihood of millions of 
Karigar's. Charging 18 percentage GST on tailoring and subsequent intermediary 
processes will substantially increase the price of the product. 
 
We request your office to reconsider the levy on Stitching cloth/ Tailoring/ Job work 
of garments and to bring it down to 5 percent from 18 percent. We hope that you 
shall receive our representation favorably and issue a notification on similar lines.  
 
We believe such a move would surely bear fruitful results in both, supporting the 
industry as well as taking forth the objectives of the government. 
 
Awaiting your response.
